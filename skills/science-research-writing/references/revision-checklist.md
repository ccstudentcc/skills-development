# Revision Checklist

## Structure

- Does the section match the rhetorical job of its genre and location?
- Does each paragraph perform one clear communicative function?
- Does the first sentence of each paragraph orient the reader?
- Is the contribution, limitation, or implication explicit where the reader needs it?

## Evidence Handling

- Are data presented with interpretation rather than as bare numbers?
- Are causal verbs, novelty claims, and certainty levels supported by the evidence?
- Are comparisons with prior work labeled clearly as confirmation, extension, contradiction, or complement?

## Sentence Control

- Does each sentence begin from something the reader already knows?
- Is the connection between adjacent sentences explicit?
- Are tense changes purposeful?
- Is ownership clear whenever passive voice appears?
- Are articles and pronouns precise?

## Terminology

- Is the same concept named the same way throughout the passage?
- Have ambiguous shorthand labels, acronym overload, or unexplained synonyms been removed?

## Final Read

- Can a reader skim paragraph openings and still recover the logic of the passage?
- Does any sentence force the reader to infer why the information matters?
- Does the prose remain faithful to the supplied evidence, numbers, and citations?
